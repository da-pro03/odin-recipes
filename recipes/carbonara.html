Carbonara

Ingredients

  • Meat: Guanciale (cured pork jowl) is traditional, but pancetta or thick-cut bacon are common substitutes if guanciale is unavailable
  • Cheese: Pecorino Romano provides a sharp, salty flavor, though Parmigiano Reggiano can be used or mixed in for a nuttier taste.
  • Eggs: The sauce relies on whole eggs and extra yolks to achieve a rich, creamy texture without scrambling.
  • Pasta: Spaghetti or bucatini are the standard choices, cooked al dente.

Preparation

  1. Cook the meat: Fry diced guanciale, pancetta, or bacon until crispy, then remove the meat and set aside, leaving the fat in the pan
  2. Prepare the sauce: Whisk together eggs (whole and yolks), grated Pecorino Romano, and coarse black pepper in a bowl; optionally temper with a splash of hot pasta water
  3. Combine: Toss the hot, drained pasta directly into the pan with the pork fat, then remove the pan from the heat to prevent scrambling
  4. Emulsify: Pour the egg mixture into the pasta while tossing vigorously, adding reserved starchy pasta water gradually until the sauce is glossy and creamy
  5. Serve: Mix in the crispy pork and serve immediately with extra cheese and pepper
